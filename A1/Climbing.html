When for climbing?

The offical climbing season is july to september. In this period,Mount Fuji suspose won't have snow. As the weather is relatively warm, it is more suitable for those people without climbing experience.

  • Yoshida climbing route: July 1st to September 10th
  • Suzouguchi, Gotemba, Fujinomiyaguchi climbing routes: July 10th to September 10th

How to climb

The path of Mount Fuji is divided into 10 stations. The first station is at the foot of the mountain and the 10th station is at the top of the mountain. The paved road is as far as the fifth station, which is about 1,400 to 2,400 meters above sea level, on different sides of the mountain. There are 4 fifth stops, from where most people start to climb up to the top of the mountain.

Kawaguchiko Fifth Station (Yamanashi Prefecture)

  • Altitude: about 2,300 meters
  • Uphill: 5-7 hours
  • Downhill: 3–5 hours

This is the most popular starting point for climbing to the top of the mountain. It is also the fifth station that is the easiest to enter from the Fuji Five Lakes area and the center of Tokyo to the fifth station. The Fuji Subaru Line is a toll road (2060 yen). The peak period of summer vacation is not open to private cars (July 10 to September 10). The Yoshidaguchi Trail is a trail that leads from the fifth station of Lake Kawaguchi to the top of the mountain. There are many mountain huts near the seventh and eighth stations. It is built along the mountain trail, and the trails for uphill and downhill are separated, and the sunrise will appear on this side of the mountain.

Suzouguchi Fifth Station (Shizuoka Prefecture)

  • Altitude: about 2,000 meters
  • Uphill: 5-8 hours
  • Downhill: 3–5 hours

This fifth station is only 2,000 meters above sea level, and it is the starting point of the trail that must be crossed. The Suzoukou Trail meets the Yoshidakou Trail near the eighth station.
